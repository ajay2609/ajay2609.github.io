## About Me


I am a radio astronomer with a deep fascination for highly energetic events in the Universe. My research primarily focuses on **Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs)**, one of the most mysterious phenomena in modern astronomy. These brief but powerful flashes of radio waves from distant galaxies continue to puzzle scientists. My work revolves around understanding their emission and developing techniques for better follow-up campaingn with different telescopes to unravel their msytery. I employ use of Deep Learning to understand the different distinct morphologies of FRBs.  

In addition to FRBs, I actively work on searching for and understanding **pulsars** – rapidly rotating neutron stars that emit beams of electromagnetic radiation. These can be imagined as lighthouses periodically cutting our line of sight. They provide unique insights into extreme physics and the nature of matter under extereme conditions impossible to replicate on Earth.
